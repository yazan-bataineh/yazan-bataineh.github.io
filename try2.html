Travel to jordan

Jordan is a hidden gem of the Middle East that combines ancient history, rich culture, and breathtaking natural landscapes.
From the golden deserts of Wadi Rum to the healing waters of the Dead Sea, from the rose-red city of Petra to the warm hospitality of its people, Jordan offers an unforgettable experience like no other.
Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a history buff, a foodie, or simply looking to relax, Jordan has something magical for you — all within reach.

Top Tourist Attractions in Jordan

Jordan is a land of contrasts, where you can explore ancient ruins, climb majestic mountains, and relax by serene waters — all in one trip. These are the must-see destinations across the country:

  • Petra – The Rose-Red City

    • Discover one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, carved into pink sandstone cliffs by the Nabataeans over 2,000 years ago. Walk through the Siq, and be amazed by the iconic Treasury and ancient tombs.

  • Wadi Rum – Valley of the Moon

    • A dramatic desert landscape of red dunes and towering rock formations. Ride a camel, take a jeep tour, or sleep under the stars in a Bedouin camp.

  • The Dead Sea

    • Float effortlessly in the salty waters of the Earth’s lowest point, and treat yourself to rejuvenating mineral mud — a natural spa experience like no other.

  • Amman – The Vibrant Capital

    • A city that blends the old with the new. Explore Roman ruins, bustling markets, trendy cafés, and a lively cultural scene

Jordanian Cuisine

Jordanian food is a flavorful journey into tradition, hospitality, and love for home-cooked meals. With dishes passed down through generations, Jordan’s cuisine offers something hearty and delicious for everyone.

  • Mansaf

    • Literally means “upside-down.” A savory one-pot dish of rice, meat, and fried vegetables flipped dramatically before serving — both a visual and flavorful delight!

  • Maqluba

    • Literally means “upside-down.” A savory one-pot dish of rice, meat, and fried vegetables flipped dramatically before serving — both a visual and flavorful delight!

  • Knafeh

    • A sweet cheese-based dessert soaked in syrup and topped with crispy vermicelli — famously made in Nablus and loved all over Jordan.

  • Hummus & Falafel

    • A beloved everyday breakfast, perfect for a light, vegetarian-friendly meal. Creamy hummus and crunchy falafel are street food staples across Jordan.

Culture & Traditions

Jordan’s culture is a vibrant mosaic of ancient heritage and warm hospitality. From Bedouin traditions to folk music and colorful crafts, every part of the country has its own story to tell.

  • Hospitality & Generosity

    • Welcoming guests is a sacred value in Jordanian society. Expect to be offered coffee, tea, or even a full meal — it’s part of the culture!

  • Traditional Dress

    • The Jordanian dress, especially in rural areas, is richly embroidered and symbolic. The red-checkered headscarf (keffiyeh) represents pride and identity.

  • Dabke – Traditional Dance

    • A popular folk dance performed at weddings and celebrations, full of energy and unity, danced in rows with synchronized footwork.

  • Festivals

    • Jordan hosts cultural events like the Jerash Festival, where music, poetry, and art bring history to life.

Where to Stay in Jordan

Whether you're traveling on a budget or looking for luxury, Jordan offers a wide range of accommodation to suit your travel style — all with a warm, welcoming touch.

  • Luxury Resorts & Hotels

    • Found in Amman, the Dead Sea, and Aqaba — offering five-star amenities, pools, spas, and stunning views.

  • Bedouin Camps in Wadi Rum

    • Stay in a traditional desert camp, enjoy homemade meals, and listen to Bedouin stories by the fire under the stars.

  • Guesthouses in Rural Villages

    • Experience local life in cozy, family-run guesthouses in places like Umm Qais and Ajloun. Perfect for slow travel and cultural immersion.

Suggested Travel Itineraries

  • 3-Day Southern Adventure (Petra & Wadi Rum)
    1. Day 1: Explore Petra
    2. Day 2: Head to Wadi Rum for desert tours

    • Visit the Baptism Site (Bethany Beyond the Jordan), Mount Nebo, and the mosaics of Madaba — all steeped in spiritual history.
